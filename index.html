Content Repurposing Funnel

This diagram illustrates how a single piece of cornerstone content is repurposed across multiple platforms to attract, engage, and convert leads into sales.

Stage 1: Create Cornerstone Content

Start with a single, high-value piece of content that serves as the foundation for the entire campaign. This asset establishes authority and provides substantial data.

Example: "The Dubai Q3 Luxury Real Estate Market Report"

Stage 2: Repurpose & Distribute Across Platforms

Break down the cornerstone content into smaller, platform-specific formats to maximize reach and engage audiences where they are most active.

üìù Blog Post

A 1,200-word summary of the report's key findings, optimized for SEO. Published on the Novvi Properties website.

üìä Infographic

Visually represents the most compelling stats (e.g., price appreciation in Dubai Marina). Shared on Pinterest and LinkedIn.

üìπ Short-Form Video

A 60-second video with an agent discussing the #1 trend from the report. Posted on Instagram Reels & TikTok.

üìß Email Newsletter

An exclusive "first look" at the infographic sent to the subscriber list, encouraging them to read the full blog post.

üí¨ Social Media Snippets

Key quotes, stats, and questions from the report are turned into daily posts for LinkedIn, Twitter, and Facebook.

üé§ Podcast Segment

A 5-minute segment in a real estate podcast discussing the report's most surprising finding.

Stage 3: Drive Traffic to a Central Hub

All repurposed content includes a clear Call-to-Action (CTA) directing traffic back to a dedicated landing page where users can access the full cornerstone asset.

Action: "Download the Full Q3 Market Report"

Users exchange their contact information (name, email, investment interest) via a form to get the complete report.

üå±

Stage 4: Nurture & Qualify Leads

Once a lead is captured, they enter an automated nurturing sequence within the CRM. The goal is to build trust and qualify their interest.

Investor Nurture Sequence:

  • Email 1: Delivers the requested market report.
  • Email 2 (3 days later): A case study on a successful investment.
  • Email 3 (1 week later): Invitation to a webinar on "Maximizing Rental Yields".
ü§ù

Stage 5: Sales Handoff & Conversion

Leads that show high engagement (e.g., attend the webinar) are flagged as Marketing Qualified Leads (MQLs) and handed off to the sales team for personalized follow-up.

Action: Sales Team Follow-Up

An agent contacts the qualified lead to offer a personalized consultation, ultimately leading to a property viewing and sale.

Operational Framework (SOP)

To ensure consistency and quality, our content operations are guided by a Standard Operating Procedure. Expand the sections below to view key workflows and responsibilities.

  • Content Manager: Oversees entire content operation, approves calendar, and reports on performance.
  • Content Creator: Researches and writes content, adhering to brand voice and deadlines.
  • SEO Specialist: Conducts keyword research, provides SEO recommendations, and monitors performance.
  • Graphic Designer: Creates all visual assets, ensuring brand consistency.
  • Social Media Manager: Distributes and promotes content across social channels.

A multi-step process involving quarterly strategy reviews and monthly calendar planning to align content with strategic pillars and SEO opportunities. Each content piece begins with a detailed brief outlining objectives, keywords, and audience.

  1. Research Phase: Creator gathers data and competitor insights.
  2. Content Development: First draft is written and optimized for SEO.
  3. Review & Optimization: Manager and SEO specialist provide feedback.
  4. Visual Asset Creation: Designer creates supporting visuals.
  5. Final Approval: Manager gives final sign-off before publishing.

Content is scheduled according to the calendar after passing a pre-publication checklist. Upon publishing, the Social Media Manager executes a promotion plan across social channels and email newsletters to maximize reach and engagement.
